IoT(Internet of Things)
IoT stands for Internet of Things which means accessing and controlling daily usable equipments and devices using internet.
Q: What is an Internet of Things(IoT)?
Ans: Connecting everyday things embedded with electronic, software and sensors to internet enabling to collect and exchange data without human interaction is called as ……
The term “Things” in the Internet of Things refers to anything and everything in day to day life which is accessed or connected through the internet.
The system created by IoT has greater transparency, control, and performance.
Q: How does it work?
The entire working process of IoT starts with the device themselves such as smartphone, digital watches, electronic appliances, which securely communicate with the IoT platform. The platforms collect and analyse the data from all multiple devices and platforms and transfer the most valuable date with applications to devices.

• Artificial Intelligence- IoT makes things smart and enhances life using data.

• Sensing- The sensor device used in IoT technologies detect and measure any change in the environment and report on their status. Without sensors there could not hold and effective or true IoT environment.

• Active Engagement- IoT makes the connected technology product or service to active engagement between each other.

• Endpoint Management- It is important to be the endpoint management of all the IoT system otherwise it makes the complete failure of the system.

Sensor in IOT(Internet of Things):-
• Sensors are used in the architecture of IoT devices.
• Sensor are used for sensing things and device etc.
• Sensor attains a physical parameter and convert it into a signal suitable for processing e.g. (Electrical, Mechanical, Optical) the characteristics of any device or material to detect the presence of a particular physical quantity.
• Output of the sensor is a signal which is converted to a human readable for like changes in characterises changes in resistances, capacitance, impedance, etc.
IoT Hardware:-
• Transducer- It converts a signal from one physical structure to another.

Sensor char:-
• Static- These are where the performance criteria for the measurement of quantities the remain constant.

• Dynamic- Dynamic characteristic of a sensor represent the time response of the sensor system.

Sensor classification:-
• Passive sensor- Cannot independently sense the input. Eg – Accelerometer, soil moisture, water level and temperature sensor.
• Active sensor- Independently sense the input. Eg – Radar, Gounder, and laser altimeter sensor.
• Analog sensor- Output of the sensor is some continuous function of its input parameter.
• Digital sensor- Response in binary nature, designed to overcome the disadvantages of analog sensor. Along with the analog sensor it also comprises extra electronics for bit conversion.
• Scalar Sensor- Detects the input parameter only based on magnitude.
• Vector sensor- Response of the sensor depends on the magnitude of the direction and orientation of input parameter.

Types of Sensors:-
• Electrical-Electrical proximity sensor may be contact or non-contact.
  Simple contact sensor operates by making the sensor and the component complete on electrical circuit.
  Non-contact electrical proximity sensor rely on the electrical principles of either induction or detecting metals or capacitance for detecting non mentals as well.

• Light Sensor- It is also known as photo sensor light dependent resistor or LDR is a Simple light Sensor available today. The property of LDR is that its resistance is inversely proportional to the intensity of the ambient light i.e., when the intensity of light increases its resistance decreases and vice versa.

• Touch Sensor- Detection of something like a touch of finger or a stylus is known as touch sensor.

• Mechanical Sensor- Any suitable mechanical electrical switch may be adopted but because a certain amount of force is required to operate a mechanical switch it is common to user micro switches.

• Pneumatic sensor- These proximity sensors operate by braking or disturbing an air flow.

• Optical sensor- these operate by breaking a light beam which falls onto a light sensitive device such as a photocell. These are examples of non-contact sensor.

• Speed sensor- sensor used for detecting the speed of any object or vehicle which is n motion is known as speed sensor.

• Temperature sensor- Devices which monitor and tracks the temperature and gives temperature measurement as an electrical signal are termed as temperature sensor.

• PIR sensor- it stands for Passive Infrared Sensor and it is an electronic sensor that is used for the tracking and measurement of infrared (IR) light radiating from objects, in its field of view and is also known as pyroelectric sensor.

• Ultrasonic sensor- in this the interpretation of echoes from radio or sound waves to evolve the attributes of a targets by generating the high frequency sound waves.

5-layer architecture of IoT-
5 layer model can be considered as an extension to the basic architecture of IoT.

• Perception layer- this is the first layer of IoT architecture. In the perception layer number of sensors and actuators are used to gather useful info like temp, moisture content, intruder detection, sounds, etc. the main function of the layer is to get information from surrounding and to pass data to another layer so that some action can be done based on that info.

• Network layer- It is the connecting layer between perception and middleware layer. It gets data from perception layer and passes data to middleware layer using networking tech like 3G, 4G, Wi-Fi, infrared, etc. all transfer of data done securely keeping the obtained data confidential.

• Middleware layer- it has some advanced feature like storage, computation, processing, action taking capabilities. It stores all data set and bases on the device address and name it gives appropriate data to that device. It can also take decisions based on calculations done on dataset obtained from sensors.

• Application layer- it managers all application process based on info obtained from middleware layer. This application alarm security emails, activating alarm, security system, turn on or off a device, smart watch, smart agriculture, etc.

• Business layer- the success of any device does not depend only on technologies used in it out also how it is being delivered to its consumer. Business layer does these tasks for the device. It involves making flowcharts, graphs, analysis of results and how device can be improved, etc.

Industrial IoT-
It is an ecosystem of devices, sensor, applications and associated networking equipment that work together to collect monitor and analyse data from industrial operations. Analysis of such data help increase visibility and enhances troubleshooting and maintenance capabilities. The most prominent examples of industrial IoT devices in manufacture are in automation remote monitoring, supply chain, optimization, digital twins, and predictive maintenance.
Q: Where is Industrial IoT used?
Ans: Industrial organization that relies on quality monitoring process can apply IoT to some effect. Chemical processing plants or pharmaceutical companies can use these sensors to monitor material or product quality remotely.
